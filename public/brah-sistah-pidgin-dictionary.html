The Complete Pidgin Dictionary of 'Brah' and 'Sistah'

Master the Most Versatile Words in Hawaiian Pidgin

From casual greetings to deep conversations - your complete guide to 'brah' and 'sistah'

If you only learn two words in Hawaiian Pidgin, make it 'brah' and 'sistah'. These aren't just words - they're the foundation of local communication, expressing everything from casual greetings to deep respect and affection. Whether you're visiting Hawaii or want to connect with local culture, this complete guide will teach you every way to use these essential Hawaiian slang terms.

Below you'll find 20+ authentic phrases with pronunciations, meanings, and real conversation examples. Let's get started!

Basic 'Brah' - Essential Greetings

1️⃣ Eh brah

Pronunciation: EH BRAH

Meaning: "Hey man" - the most basic greeting

Usage: Universal greeting for any guy, casual and friendly

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Eh brah, long time!"
Person B: "Shoots! How you stay?"

2️⃣ Howzit brah

Pronunciation: HOW-zit BRAH

Meaning: "How's it going, man?"

Usage: Friendly check-in with someone you know

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Howzit brah?"
Person B: "Good good, just pau work."

3️⃣ Shoots brah

Pronunciation: SHOOTS BRAH

Meaning: "Okay man" or "Sounds good, bro"

Usage: Agreement or acknowledgment, super common

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Meet you at da beach, 3 o'clock?"
Person B: "Shoots brah, see you there."

4️⃣ Da kine brah

Pronunciation: dah KYNE BRAH

Meaning: "You know what I mean, man" - the thing, that guy

Usage: When you can't remember the exact word or name

Example Conversation:

Person A: "You know da kine brah, da one wit da red truck?"
Person B: "Oh yeah yeah, Kenny."

Advanced 'Brah' - Expressing Emotion

5️⃣ Ho brah

Pronunciation: HO BRAH

Meaning: Expression of surprise, amazement, or disbelief

Usage: When something unexpected or impressive happens

Example Conversation:

Person A: "I wen catch one 15-pound mahi!"
Person B: "Ho brah! Das huge!"

6️⃣ Brah, fo' real?

Pronunciation: BRAH, foh REE-ul

Meaning: "Man, are you serious?" or "Really, bro?"

Usage: Questioning or confirming something surprising

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Eh, Kimo going move mainland."
Person B: "Brah, fo' real? When?"

7️⃣ No can brah

Pronunciation: NO can BRAH

Meaning: "I can't, man" or "That's not possible, bro"

Usage: Declining an offer or stating inability

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Come beach today?"
Person B: "No can brah, gotta work."

8️⃣ Mahalo brah

Pronunciation: mah-HA-lo BRAH

Meaning: "Thanks man" - mixing Hawaiian with Pidgin

Usage: Showing gratitude in a local way

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Here, I brought extra musubi for you."
Person B: "Mahalo brah! I was starving!"

9️⃣ Brah, what?!

Pronunciation: BRAH, waht?!

Meaning: "Man, what?!" - shock or disbelief

Usage: When something is unbelievable or outrageous

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Police wen shut down da whole beach!"
Person B: "Brah, what?! Why?"

Sorry brah

Pronunciation: SOR-ry BRAH

Meaning: "Sorry man" - sincere apology

Usage: Apologizing or expressing sympathy

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Sorry brah, I wen forget pick you up."
Person B: "No worries, happens."

Basic 'Sistah' - Greetings & Connection

1️⃣1️⃣ Eh sistah

Pronunciation: EH SIS-tah

Meaning: "Hey girl" - friendly greeting

Usage: Universal greeting for any woman, warm and familiar

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Eh sistah! Long time no see!"
Person B: "I know! We gotta catch up!"

1️⃣2️⃣ Howzit sistah

Pronunciation: HOW-zit SIS-tah

Meaning: "How are you, girl?"

Usage: Checking in with a friend or acquaintance

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Howzit sistah? You good?"
Person B: "Staying good! Just busy wit work."

1️⃣3️⃣ Thanks sistah

Pronunciation: TANKS SIS-tah

Meaning: "Thanks girl" - grateful and warm

Usage: Expressing gratitude with affection

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Here, I brought you some lau lau."
Person B: "Thanks sistah! You're the best!"

1️⃣4️⃣ Love you sistah

Pronunciation: LUV you SIS-tah

Meaning: "Love you girl" - affectionate farewell or expression

Usage: Deep friendship and sisterhood, very common

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Okay girl, I gotta go. See you tomorrow!"
Person B: "Love you sistah! Drive safe!"

1️⃣5️⃣ My sistah

Pronunciation: my SIS-tah

Meaning: "My girl" or "my sister" - possessive endearment

Usage: Introducing or referring to a close friend

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Come, I like you meet my sistah Lani."
Person B: "Nice to meet you!"

Advanced 'Sistah' - Deep Connection

1️⃣6️⃣ Ho sistah

Pronunciation: HO SIS-tah

Meaning: Expression of surprise, sympathy, or excitement

Usage: Reacting to news or events with emotion

Example Conversation:

Person A: "I wen get da job!"
Person B: "Ho sistah! Congrats!"

1️⃣7️⃣ Sistah, you okay?

Pronunciation: SIS-tah, you o-KAY?

Meaning: "Girl, are you alright?" - showing concern

Usage: Checking on someone who seems troubled

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Sistah, you okay? You stay look sad."
Person B: "Yeah, just having one hard day."

1️⃣8️⃣ Shoots sistah

Pronunciation: SHOOTS SIS-tah

Meaning: "Okay girl" or "Sounds good, sis"

Usage: Agreement or acknowledgment between women

Example Conversation:

Person A: "We go shopping Saturday?"
Person B: "Shoots sistah! What time?"

1️⃣9️⃣ Auntie (honorary sistah)

Pronunciation: AHN-tee

Meaning: Respectful term for older women, like "sistah" with honor

Usage: Addressing or referring to older women with respect

Example Conversation:

Person A: "Thanks for da food, Auntie!"
Person B: "No problem, keiki. Come back anytime."

2️⃣0️⃣ My sista from anotha mista

Pronunciation: my SIS-tah from ah-NOH-thah MIS-tah

Meaning: "My sister from another mister" - playful term for close friend

Usage: Emphasizing close friendship despite not being blood-related

Example Conversation:

Person A: "This my sista from anotha mista, we tight since small kid time."
Person B: "Aww, das so sweet!"

Cultural Context: Why 'Brah' and 'Sistah' Matter

'Brah' and 'sistah' aren't just Hawaiian versions of "bro" and "sister" - they represent the ohana (family) spirit that defines Hawaiian culture. These words create instant connection and belonging, whether you're blood-related or not.

In Hawaii, everyone is family. Calling someone 'brah' or 'sistah' acknowledges this cultural truth - we're all connected, we all belong to this place, and we look out for each other. It's not about being informal or casual; it's about recognizing our shared humanity and community.

When to use them:

  • Brah: Use with men of any age in casual settings. Shows friendliness and respect simultaneously.
  • Sistah: Use with women to create warmth and connection. More personal than "miss" or "ma'am".
  • Uncle/Auntie: For older folks (roughly 50+), showing additional respect while maintaining warmth.

Remember: These words are invitations to community. Use them with genuine aloha spirit, and you'll be welcomed into the local family.

Pro Tips for Using 'Brah' and 'Sistah'

DO: Use with Warmth

These words should always come from a place of genuine connection and respect.

DO: Match the Energy

If locals are being casual, join in! If the vibe is more formal, adjust accordingly.

DO: Combine with Other Pidgin

"Shoots brah" and "Mahalo sistah" feel more authentic than using them alone.

DON'T: Force It

If it doesn't feel natural, don't use it. Locals can tell when it's not authentic.

DON'T: Use Sarcastically

These words carry weight. Using them mockingly is disrespectful to local culture.

DON'T: Assume Gender

While 'brah' is typically for men and 'sistah' for women, follow the person's lead.

BRAH ESSENTIALS

  • Greeting: Eh brah, Howzit brah
  • Agreement: Shoots brah, Can brah
  • Surprise: Ho brah, Brah what?!
  • Decline: No can brah, Sorry brah
  • Thanks: Mahalo brah, Thanks brah

SISTAH ESSENTIALS

  • Greeting: Eh sistah, Howzit sistah
  • Agreement: Shoots sistah, Can sistah
  • Concern: Sistah you okay?, Ho sistah
  • Affection: Love you sistah, My sistah
  • Thanks: Thanks sistah, Mahalo sistah
