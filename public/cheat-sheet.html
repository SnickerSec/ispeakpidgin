HAWAIIAN PIDGIN

Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

Essential Grammar • Phrases • Vocabulary | ChokePidgin.com

GRAMMAR RULES

"STAY" = Am/Is/Are
I stay tired • Da food stay ono
"WEN" = Past Tense
I wen go store • She wen eat
"GOING" = Will/Future
I going call you • We going beach
"DA" = The | "ONE" = A/An
Da waves • One car • Da kine
Pronouns: Dey/Dem/Dat/Dis
They→Dey • Them→Dem • That→Dat
Negation: "No" / "Neva"
Don't→No • Didn't→Neva • Can't→No can

GREETINGS & RESPONSES

PRO TIPS

  • • "Da kine" is the most versatile word - use it for anything!
  • • Drop consonants: "Little bit" → "Litto bit"
  • • Th → D: "This" → "Dis", "That" → "Dat"
  • • Respect elders by using "Auntie" or "Uncle"
  • • Don't force it - understanding is more important than speaking
  • • Learn more at ChokePidgin.com

EXAMPLE CONVERSATIONS

At the Beach

A: "Howzit brah!"

B: "Shoots! Da waves stay choke today!"

A: "Yeah! Going surf bumbye?"

B: "Can! Try wait, I going grab my board."

Getting Food 🍜

A: "Eh, you wen eat yet?"

B: "Nah, I stay hungry!"

A: "Let's go grind. I know one ono place."

B: "Shoots! Da kine broke da mouth?"

A: "Yeah brah, choke ono!"

After Work 💼

A: "You pau hana already?"

B: "Yeah, finally pau! I stay tired."

A: "Going talk story bumbye?"

B: "Can! Meet you makai side."
