What Does "Ono" Mean?

The Hawaiian Word for Delicious Food

Quick Answer

"Ono" (pronounced "OH-no") means delicious, tasty, or yummy in Hawaiian. It's the essential word for describing great food in Hawaii. You'll see it everywhere - on menus, food truck names, restaurant signs, and in everyday conversation. When something tastes amazing, locals say it's "ono!" Fun fact: "Ono" is also the name of a popular fish (wahoo) - named for how delicious it is!

How to Pronounce "Ono"

OH-no

Breakdown:

  • "OH" - Like "oh" in "go" or "so"
  • "no" - Like "no" as in "no problem"

Sounds like: "Oh no!" (like you dropped something) - but said with equal emphasis on both syllables and a positive tone!

Common mistake: Don't say "AH-no" or "OO-no" - keep both vowels as clear "oh" sounds.

How to Use "Ono"

Describing Food

"Dis poke stay ono!"

This poke is delicious!

Asking About Food

"Da food ono or what?"

Is the food good?

Restaurant Names

"Ono Hawaiian Foods"

Common name for restaurants

With "Grindz"

"Dat's some ono grindz!"

That's some delicious food!

"Ono Grindz" - The Classic Combo

"Ono grindz" (or "ono grinds") is one of the most common phrases in Hawaii. "Grindz" is Pidgin for food (from "grinding" = eating), so "ono grindz" literally means "delicious food."

You'll see "Ono Grindz" on food trucks, restaurant signs, and hear it in everyday conversation. When someone asks "Wea get da ono grindz?" they're asking where to find good food!

Real Examples

"Ho, dis laulau stay so ono!"

Wow, this laulau is so delicious!

"Aunty's cooking always ono"

Aunty's cooking is always delicious

"You gotta try da garlic shrimp - so ono!"

You have to try the garlic shrimp - it's so good!

"Da malasadas from Leonard's stay ono!"

The malasadas from Leonard's are delicious!

"Not bad, but not dat ono"

It's okay, but not that great

Ono - The Fish

"Ono" is also the Hawaiian name for wahoo - a popular game fish found in Hawaiian waters. The fish got its name because it's so delicious! Ono fish has firm, white flesh and is prized for grilling, searing, or making into poke.

So yes, you can say "Da ono stay ono!" (The wahoo is delicious!) - and locals will know exactly what you mean!
