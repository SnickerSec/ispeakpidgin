15 Essential Pidgin Phrases for Ordering Plate Lunch

Order Like a Local at Your Favorite Hawaiian Plate Lunch Spot

Why Learn Pidgin for Plate Lunch?

Walking into a local Hawaiian plate lunch spot can be intimidating if you don't know the lingo. But mastering just a few essential Hawaiian Pidgin phrases will make you sound like a local, earn respect from the staff, and help you get exactly what you want from your meal.

Whether you're visiting Hawaii or you're a new resident, this guide will teach you 15 authentic phrases you'll hear (and need to use) at every plate lunch counter from Honolulu to Hilo.

Pro Tip

Plate lunch is more than just food in Hawaii—it's a cultural institution. Using Pidgin shows respect for local culture and makes your dining experience more authentic and enjoyable.

The Essential 15 Phrases

1️⃣ GRINDZ

"Grindz"

Meaning: Food (noun) or to eat (verb)

Usage: "Da grindz stay ono!" (The food is delicious!) or "We going grind!" (We're going to eat!)

Example conversation:

"Eh, where get good grindz around here?"

2️⃣ TWO SCOOP RICE

"Two scoop rice"

Meaning: The standard serving at plate lunch - two scoops of white rice

Usage: This is the default, but you can say "One scoop rice" or "Three scoop rice" if you want more or less.

Example conversation:

"Can get da chicken katsu wit' two scoop rice?"

3️⃣ OH-no

"Ono"

Meaning: Delicious, tasty

Usage: Use this to describe food that tastes amazing. "Dis plate stay ono!" = "This plate is delicious!"

Example conversation:

"Da loco moco stay ono, brah!"

4️⃣ can GET...

"Can get...?"

Meaning: Can I have...? / Do you have...?

Usage: The most common way to order. "Can get one plate?" = "Can I have a plate lunch?"

Example conversation:

"Can get da mixed plate wit' extra mac salad?"

5️⃣ CHOKE

"Choke"

Meaning: A lot, plenty

Usage: "Choke mac salad" = lots of mac salad. "Get choke meat" = has plenty of meat.

Example conversation:

"Can put choke gravy on top?"

6️⃣ PAU

"Pau"

Meaning: Finished, done, all gone

Usage: "Pau already?" = Are you done? / "Da kalbi pau" = The kalbi is sold out.

Example conversation:

"You pau wit' your plate? I going order."

7️⃣ dah KYNE

"Da kine"

Meaning: The thing, that thing, whatchamacallit

Usage: Universal filler word. "Can get da kine?" while pointing = Can I get that?

Example conversation:

"I like da kine chicken wit' da kine sauce."

8️⃣ foh TAKE-out

"For take out" / "To go"

Meaning: Food packaged to take away

Usage: Always specify if you're eating there or taking away. "One plate, for take out."

Example conversation:

"Can get two mixed plate, for go?"

9️⃣ can PUT...

"Can put...?"

Meaning: Can you add...?

Usage: Ask for additions or modifications. "Can put extra gravy?" = Can you add extra gravy?

Example conversation:

"Can put da sauce on da side?"

no GET...

"No get..."

Meaning: Don't have, out of stock

Usage: You'll hear this when items are sold out. "No get teri beef" = We don't have teriyaki beef.

Example conversation:

"Sorry, no get poke today."

1️⃣1️⃣ BROKE dah MOUTH

"Broke da mouth"

Meaning: So delicious it's amazing (literal: broke the mouth)

Usage: The highest compliment for food. "Dis kalua pig stay broke da mouth!"

Example conversation:

"Brah, dat loco moco was broke da mouth good!"

1️⃣2️⃣ SHOOTS

"Shoots"

Meaning: Okay, sounds good, yes

Usage: Confirm your order or agree. "Shoots, tanks" = Okay, thank you.

Example conversation:

"Shoots, I take one mixed plate den."

1️⃣3️⃣ how MUCH?

"How much?"

Meaning: What's the price?

Usage: Simple and direct. Just ask "How much?" when checking prices.

Example conversation:

"How much for da mini plate?"

1️⃣4️⃣ TANKS / mah-HAH-lo

"Tanks" / "Mahalo"

Meaning: Thank you

Usage: Both work! "Tanks" is Pidgin slang, "Mahalo" is Hawaiian. Use either.

Example conversation:

"Tanks ah! Da grindz stay ono!"

1️⃣5️⃣ no NEED...

"No need..."

Meaning: Don't need, I don't want

Usage: Decline items you don't want. "No need mac salad" = I don't want mac salad.

Example conversation:

"Can get da teriyaki chicken, but no need da rice."

Putting It All Together: Sample Order

Complete Plate Lunch Order in Pidgin:

You: "Howzit! Can get one mixed plate?"

Worker: "Shoots, what kind meat you like?"

You: "Can get da chicken katsu and kalbi? Two scoop rice wit' choke mac salad."

Worker: "Can put extra gravy?"

You: "Shoots! And for take out. How much?"

Worker: "$12.50"

You: "Tanks ah!"
