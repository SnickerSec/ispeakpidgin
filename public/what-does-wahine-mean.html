What Does "Wahine" Mean?

The Hawaiian Word for Woman

Quick Answer

"Wahine" (pronounced "wah-HEE-nay") is the Hawaiian word for woman or female. You'll encounter it everywhere in Hawaii - on bathroom signs (Wahine/Kane), in surfing culture (wahine surfers), and as a term of respect for women of all ages. It's one of the most commonly used Hawaiian words in daily island life.

How to Pronounce "Wahine"

wah-HEE-nay

Breakdown:

  • "wah" - Like "wa" in "water"
  • "HEE" - Like "he" in "he" (stressed syllable)
  • "nay" - Like "nay" as in "neigh"

Memory tip: It rhymes with "bikini" - wah-HEE-nay!

Common mistake: Don't say "wah-HINE" or "WAH-heen" - keep the emphasis on HEE.

The Meaning of "Wahine"

1

Basic Meaning

Wahine simply means woman, female, or wife in Hawaiian. It can refer to women of any age, though sometimes "wahine" implies a young woman while older women may be called "kupuna wahine" (elder woman).

2

On Bathroom Signs

Throughout Hawaii, you'll see "Wahine" (women's restroom) and "Kane" (men's restroom) on bathroom doors. It's one of the first Hawaiian words visitors learn out of necessity!

3

In Surfing Culture

In surf culture, "wahine" refers to female surfers. Surf competitions often have "wahine divisions," and the term is used globally to describe women surfers. Hawaii's legendary wahine surfers helped pioneer the sport.

Wahine vs. Kane

How to Use "Wahine" - Examples

"Where's the wahine restroom?"

Asking for the women's bathroom

"She's one mean wahine surfer!"

Complimenting a skilled female surfer

"My wahine stay at home today."

My wife/woman is at home today

"The wahine division starts at noon."

Women's competition begins at noon

Cultural Significance

In Hawaiian culture, women (wahine) hold a place of great respect and importance. Historically, certain kapu (taboos) protected women, and female ali'i (royalty) wielded significant power. The goddess Pele is one of Hawaii's most powerful deities. Today, "wahine" is used with pride and respect - whether describing athletes, professionals, mothers, or any woman living with aloha spirit.
