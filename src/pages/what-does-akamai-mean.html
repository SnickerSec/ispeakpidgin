What Does "Akamai" Mean? 🧠

The Hawaiian Word for Smart and Clever

Quick Answer

"Akamai" (pronounced "ah-KAH-my") is a Hawaiian word that means "smart," "clever," or "intelligent." While it often refers to intellectual brightness, it can also describe someone who is technically skilled, wise, or practical in their thinking.

More Than Just "Smart"

In Hawaii, calling someone "akamai" is a high compliment. It implies a sense of competence and capability. It’s the opposite of "lolo" (crazy or stupid). You might hear it used to describe a student who gets good grades, a mechanic who can fix anything, or even a dog that knows plenty of tricks.

The word is so well-known that it's even used as the name for one of the world's largest content delivery networks (Akamai Technologies)!

How to Pronounce

ah-KAH-my

Breakdown:

  • "A" - sounds like "ah" (as in "father")
  • "ka" - sounds like "KAH" (emphasis here)
  • "mai" - sounds like "my"

Real Examples

Complimenting a child

"Ho, your keiki stay so akamai! He already know his ABCs."

Translation: "Wow, your child is so smart! He already knows his ABCs."

Solving a problem

"Dass one akamai way fo' fix 'um!"

Translation: "That is a clever way to fix it!"

Related Words

Lolo

The opposite of akamai—meaning crazy, stupid, or silly.

Keiki

Meaning child—often used with akamai.

Mahalo

Thank you—what you say to an akamai person who helps you!
