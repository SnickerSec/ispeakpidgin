What Does "Amped" Mean? ⚡

Hawaiian Pidgin Slang for Excited and Energized

Quick Answer

"Amped" is a popular slang term in Hawaiian Pidgin that means "excited," "energized," or "stoked." When someone says they stay "amped," it means they have high energy and are eagerly anticipating something, like a big swell for surfing or a fun gathering.

Getting Amped in Hawaii

While the word "amped" originates from "amplified," in the islands, it’s a core part of the local vocabulary. It describes that feeling of pure adrenaline and anticipation. You might hear it used to describe a person's state of mind or the energy of a crowd.

Common Examples:

  • "I stay so amped fo go surf today, da waves look mean!"
  • "Brah, you stay amped up or wat? Relax!"
  • "Everybody stay amped fo da concert tonight."

Related Pidgin Terms

Shaka

The ultimate island gesture of "stoked" and "aloha."

Shoots

"Alright" or "Let's do it!" often said when amped.

Akamai

Being smart and clever—knowing when to get amped!
