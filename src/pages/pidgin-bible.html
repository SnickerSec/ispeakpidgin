About Da Jesus Book

Da Jesus Book is the complete New Testament translated into authentic Hawaiian Pidgin (Hawaiian Creole English). This landmark translation project was completed by Wycliffe Bible Translators to bring scripture to the hundreds of thousands of people in Hawaii who speak Pidgin as their heart language.

For many locals in Hawaii, reading the Bible in standard English feels distant and formal. Da Jesus Book brings scripture to life in the way people actually talk‚Äîmaking God's word more accessible, relatable, and meaningful to Hawaii's multicultural community.

This translation demonstrates that Hawaiian Pidgin is a legitimate language worthy of carrying sacred texts, not just casual conversation. It validates the language and culture of Hawaii's local community.

Sample Verses in Pidgin

John 3:16

"God get so plenny love an aloha fo da peopo inside da world, dat he wen send me, his one an ony Boy, so dat everybody dat trus me no get cut off from God, but get da kine life dat stay to da max foeva."

Standard English: "For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life."

Matthew 5:3 (Beatitudes)

"God goin do plenny good tings fo da peopo dat know inside dey need God, cuz God goin be dea King."

Standard English: "Blessed are the poor in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven."

Psalm 23:1

"Da Boss Up Dea, he take care me jalike da sheep farma guy take care his sheeps. I get everyting I need."

Standard English: "The Lord is my shepherd, I shall not want."

1 Corinthians 13:4 (Love Chapter)

"Wen peopo get love an aloha fo odda peopo, dey stay good inside, dey wait. Dey do good tings fo da odda peopo. Dey no stay jealous. Dey no brag. Dey no ack high makamaka."

Standard English: "Love is patient, love is kind. It does not envy, it does not boast, it is not proud."

Matthew 6:9-10 (The Lord's Prayer)

"Our Fadda inside da sky, we like all da peopo know fo shua how you stay, an dat you stay good an spesho. We like you come da King fo everybody. We like everybody make jalike you like, ova hea inside da world, jalike da angel guys up inside da sky make jalike you like."

Standard English: "Our Father in heaven, hallowed be your name, your kingdom come, your will be done, on earth as it is in heaven."

About the Translation

Wycliffe Bible Translators

Da Jesus Book was translated by Wycliffe Bible Translators, an international organization dedicated to making the Bible accessible in every language. The Hawaii Pidgin translation project took over a decade and involved:

  • Native Pidgin speakers who grew up with the language
  • Biblical scholars ensuring theological accuracy
  • Linguists who understand Hawaiian Creole English structure
  • Community reviewers from across all Hawaiian islands

Translation Philosophy

The translation team used a dynamic equivalence approach‚Äîtranslating the meaning and impact of scripture, not just word-for-word. This ensures that Pidgin speakers experience the text the same way original audiences did: clear, powerful, and culturally relevant.

First Published

The complete New Testament in Hawaiian Pidgin was published in 2000, making it one of the few complete Bible translations in a creole language. It represents a significant achievement in both linguistic and cultural preservation.
