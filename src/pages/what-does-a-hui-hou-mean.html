What Does "A Hui Hou" Mean? 🌅

The Hawaiian Phrase for "Until We Meet Again"

Quick Answer

"A Hui Hou" (pronounced "ah-hoo-ee-how") is a traditional Hawaiian phrase that means "until we meet again." Rather than a final "goodbye," it is a hopeful farewell that assumes your paths will cross again in the future.

The Warmth of A Hui Hou

In Hawaiian culture, language holds deep spiritual power (mana). Saying a final goodbye can feel abrupt or permanent. Instead, locals prefer "A Hui Hou" because it carries a promise of reconnection.

Whether you are leaving a dinner party, saying goodbye to visitors boarding a flight, or writing a parting message to a loved one, "A Hui Hou" conveys warmth, hospitality, and hope.

How to Pronounce

ah-hoo-ee-how

Pronunciation breakdown:

  • "A" - sounds like "ah" (as in "father")
  • "Hui" - sounds like "hoo-ee" (blended quickly as one syllable)
  • "Hou" - sounds like "how" (rhyming with "cow")

Real Examples

Saying farewell to friends

"A hui hou, brah! Have one safe flight back home."

Translation: "Until we meet again, friend! Have a safe flight back home."

Parting from family

"A hui hou, Tutu! We going see you next summer."

Translation: "Until we meet again, grandma/grandpa! We will see you next summer."
