What Does "Bruddah" Mean? 🤙

The Essential Island Word for Brother and Friend

Quick Answer

"Bruddah" (or "Braddah") is a staple of Hawaiian Pidgin that means "brother" or "male friend." It is used as a friendly and respectful way to address any man, whether you've known them for years or just met them.

How to Use "Bruddah"

In Hawaii, "bruddah" is more than just a word; it’s an expression of the Aloha Spirit. It creates an instant sense of community and connection. You’ll hear it at the beach, in the workplace, and everywhere in between.

Common Examples:

  • "Wassup, bruddah! How you stay?"
  • "Tanks, bruddah, I appreciate da help."
  • "Eh bruddah, you know what time da store close?"

Bruddah vs. Braddah

You might see the word spelled as both "bruddah" and "braddah." There is no real difference in meaning between the two; they are simply phonetic variations of the same Pidgin word. While "braddah" is slightly more common in written Pidgin, both are perfectly acceptable and understood by locals.

Related Pidgin Terms

Sistah

The female equivalent—used for a sister or female friend.

Howzit

The most common greeting often paired with "bruddah."

Ohana

Meaning "family"—because all bruddahs are part of the ohana!
